## 4. Supported Cities & Cultural Themes

### 4.1 Varanasi (Kashi) - The Spiritual Capital
**Cultural Identity**: Ancient spiritual center, oldest living city
**Local Nuances**:
- **Religious Sensitivity**: Extremely sacred city, dress modestly near ghats
- **Ghat Culture**: Each ghat has specific purposes (cremation, bathing, ceremonies)
- **Language**: Hindi with Bhojpuri influence, "Bhaiya/Didi" for respect
- **Timing**: Early morning (4-7 AM) is most sacred time for rituals
- **Etiquette**: Remove shoes near temples, don't point feet toward Ganga
- **Local Wisdom**: "Har Har Mahadev" greeting shows cultural awareness

### 4.2 Delhi - The Cultural Melting Pot
**Cultural Identity**: Historical capital with Mughal and British influences
**Local Nuances**:
- **Language Mix**: Hindi-English mix ("Hinglish"), Punjabi influence
- **Social Hierarchy**: More formal address systems, "Sir/Madam" common
- **Food Culture**: Heavy Punjabi influence, larger portions, wheat-based
- **Shopping**: Bargaining expected in markets, fixed prices in malls
- **Transport Etiquette**: Metro has ladies' coaches, auto-rickshaw bargaining
- **Regional Pride**: Strong connection to political/historical significance

### 4.3 Mumbai - The Commercial Heart
**Cultural Identity**: Cosmopolitan business hub, Bollywood center
**Local Nuances**:
- **Language**: Marathi base with Hindi overlay, English widely used
- **Time Culture**: Fast-paced, "Mumbai time" means punctuality matters
- **Local Transport**: Train etiquette crucial, specific coaches for ladies
- **Food Timing**: Late dining culture, street food available 24/7
- **Social Mixing**: More liberal dress codes, diverse communities
- **Local Pride**: "Mumbai spirit" of resilience and helping strangers

### 4.4 Jaipur - The Royal Heritage City
**Cultural Identity**: Rajasthani royal culture, traditional crafts
**Local Nuances**:
- **Language**: Rajasthani dialect with Hindi, "Khamma Ghani" greeting
- **Royal Etiquette**: Respect for traditional hierarchy and customs
- **Craft Culture**: Haggling is art form, quality assessment important
- **Dress Codes**: Traditional attire appreciated, especially in palaces
- **Food Culture**: Vegetarian-dominant, sweet-heavy cuisine
- **Festival Culture**: Vibrant celebrations, tourist participation welcomed

### 4.5 Rishikesh - The Yoga Capital
**Cultural Identity**: Spiritual retreat, adventure sports hub
**Local Nuances**:
- **Spiritual Etiquette**: Quiet zones near ashrams, meditation respect
- **Dress Codes**: Conservative clothing, white/saffron colors respected
- **Food Culture**: Strictly vegetarian, no alcohol in many areas
- **Language**: Hindi with spiritual Sanskrit terms commonly used
- **Activity Timing**: Early morning yoga/meditation, evening aarti
- **Environmental Respect**: Ganga conservation awareness crucial

## 5. Deep Cultural Context & Local Nuances

### 5.1 Indian Social Etiquette & Hierarchy
**Addressing People**:
- **Age Respect**: "Uncle/Aunty" for older strangers (30+)
- **Professional**: "Sir/Madam" in formal settings
- **Friendly**: "Bhai/Behen" (brother/sister) for peers
- **Regional**: "Sahab/Sahiba" in North, "Anna/Akka" in South

**Religious Sensitivity**:
- **Temple Etiquette**: Remove shoes, cover head if required, no leather
- **Mosque Visits**: Cover arms/legs, women may need head covering
- **Gurdwara Protocol**: Cover head, sit on floor, participate in langar
- **Church Visits**: Modest dress, respectful silence during services

**Social Interactions**:
- **Namaste**: Universal respectful greeting, hands together
- **Personal Space**: Less concept of personal space, normal to stand close
- **Eye Contact**: Limited with opposite gender in traditional areas
- **Photography**: Always ask permission, especially of women/religious sites

### 5.2 Regional Language Nuances & Street Phrases

**Common Hindi Street Phrases**:
- "Kitna paisa?" → "How much money?" (for shopping)
- "Thoda kam karo" → "Reduce the price a little" (bargaining)
- "Yahan kya famous hai?" → "What's famous here?" (for food/shopping)
- "Paani kahan milega?" → "Where can I get water?"
- "Bathroom kahan hai?" → "Where is the bathroom?"

**Regional Variations**:
- **North India**: More direct communication, louder conversations normal
- **South India**: More formal, English mixed with local languages
- **West India**: Business-like efficiency, time-conscious
- **East India**: Intellectual discussions, cultural conversations valued

**Respectful Phrases**:
- "Aap kaise hain?" → Formal "How are you?"
- "Dhanyawad" → More formal than "Thank you"
- "Maaf kijiye" → "Excuse me/Sorry" (polite form)
- "Kripaya" → "Please" (very respectful)

### 5.3 Street Food Culture & Eating Etiquette

**Hygiene Awareness**:
- **Safe Choices**: Hot, freshly cooked items, avoid raw salads
- **Water Safety**: Bottled water, avoid ice in street drinks
- **Vendor Selection**: Busy stalls with high turnover, local crowd presence
- **Timing**: Avoid very early morning or late night street food

**Eating Customs**:
- **Hand Usage**: Right hand for eating, left considered unclean
- **Sharing Culture**: Common to share food, offer to others first
- **Spice Levels**: Start mild, build tolerance gradually
- **Payment**: Small denominations helpful, exact change appreciated

**Regional Specialties Context**:
- **Varanasi**: Kachori-sabzi breakfast, lassi culture, seasonal malaiyo
- **Delhi**: Chaat culture, paranthas, heavy breakfast traditions
- **Mumbai**: Vada pav timing, bhel puri beaches, late-night food culture
- **Jaipur**: Dal-baati-churma, sweet lassi, royal cuisine influence
- **Rishikesh**: Sattvic food, no onion/garlic in many places, herbal teas

### 5.4 Shopping Culture & Market Dynamics

**Bargaining Etiquette**:
- **Starting Point**: Expect 30-50% markup in tourist areas
- **Negotiation Style**: Friendly, not aggressive; walk away technique works
- **Cultural Respect**: Don't insult the product, focus on price fairness
- **Final Price**: Leave some profit for vendor, don't over-bargain

**Market Timing**:
- **Best Times**: Morning (9-11 AM) for fresh items, evening (5-7 PM) for deals
- **Avoid**: Afternoon heat, lunch time (1-3 PM), very late evening
- **Festival Seasons**: Prices higher, but better variety and cultural experience

**Quality Assessment**:
- **Textiles**: Feel fabric quality, check for color fastness
- **Handicrafts**: Look for authentic vs. machine-made differences
- **Spices**: Fresh aroma, proper packaging, avoid pre-ground when possible
- **Jewelry**: Understand silver vs. white metal, gold purity standards

### 5.5 Transportation & Navigation Culture

**Auto-rickshaw Etiquette**:
- **Meter Usage**: Insist on meter in cities where mandatory
- **Negotiation**: Pre-negotiate for tourist destinations
- **Sharing**: Common in some cities, ask before getting in
- **Tipping**: Round up fare, not mandatory but appreciated

**Public Transport**:
- **Ladies Coaches**: Separate sections in metros/trains for women
- **Priority Seats**: For elderly, pregnant women, disabled
- **Rush Hours**: Avoid 8-10 AM, 6-8 PM if possible
- **Ticket Etiquette**: Keep tickets till exit, help others if needed

## 7. Cultural Sensitivity Guidelines

### 7.1 Religious Respect
- **Never mock or dismiss** religious practices, even if explaining them simply
- **Use respectful language** when discussing religious sites or customs
- **Acknowledge diversity** within religions (not all Hindus/Muslims/etc. are same)
- **Provide practical guidance** without theological explanations

### 7.2 Social Hierarchy Awareness
- **Acknowledge** that hierarchy exists without endorsing discrimination
- **Provide practical advice** for navigating social situations respectfully
- **Explain context** without making judgments about right/wrong
- **Focus on respect** and appropriate behavior for visitors

### 7.3 Economic Sensitivity
- **Avoid poverty tourism** language or recommendations
- **Respect local economics** - don't encourage unfair bargaining
- **Acknowledge** that what seems cheap to tourists supports local livelihoods
- **Promote fair exchange** rather than exploitation of price differences

### 8.1 Tone & Approach
- **Respectfully curious** rather than judgmental about differences

## 13. Non-Goals (Culturally Important)
The app is NOT:
- A platform for cultural judgment or comparison
- A tool for reinforcing stereotypes about India
- A service for finding the "cheapest" options without fair value consideration
